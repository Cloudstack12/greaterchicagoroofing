Types of Skylights

Skylights come in various shapes, sizes, and styles and can be made from different materials, making it easy to find one that fits your specific needs and preferences. Some of the most common types of skylights include:


  • Tubular Skylights are compact skylights that channel natural light from the roof to a diffuser in the ceiling. They are great for small spaces, such as closets or bathrooms.

  • Dome Skylights: These are round skylights mounted on the roof and feature a clear, curved surface that allows light to enter the room below.

  • Flat Glass Skylights: These are flat, rectangular skylights mounted on the roof and feature a clear glass surface. They are famous for living rooms, kitchens, and other large spaces.

  • Velux Skylights: These are high-quality skylights designed and manufactured by Velux, a leading manufacturer of skylights and roof windows. They come in various shapes and sizes and can be customized to fit your needs.

  • Solar-Powered Skylights: These are skylights equipped with a solar panel that converts natural light into electricity, making them a more energy-efficient option.

  • Remote-Controlled Skylights: These are skylights that can be controlled using a remote control or smartphone app, making it easy to adjust the amount of light and ventilation entering your space.

    • Each type of skylight has unique benefits and features, so you must consider your specific needs and preferences when choosing one. Whether you’re looking for a compact option for a small space, or an enormous skylight that can brighten up a large room, there is a skylight that’s right for you.

Factors to Consider Before Installing Skylights.

Installing skylights can bring natural light and fresh air into your home, creating a more comfortable and energy-efficient living space. However, before making this investment, several factors must be considered to ensure that you choose the right skylight for your home and get the most out of your investment.


  • Roof Type: The type of roof you have will determine the kind of skylight suitable for your home. For example, flat roof skylights are explicitly designed for flat roofs, while dome skylights are ideal for pitched roofs.

  • Room Location: The location of the room where you plan to install the skylight will impact the amount of sunlight and heat that will enter the room. For example, rooms facing north will receive less direct sunlight than rooms facing south.
  • Size: The size of the skylight will also impact the amount of light and heat that enters the room. More enormous skylights will allow more light and warmth, while smaller skylights will have less impact.

  • Glazing Options: There are different types of glazing available for skylights, including tempered, laminated, and Low-E glass. Each class offers other benefits, such as increased energy efficiency, UV protection, and sound insulation.

  • Energy Efficiency: Consider the energy efficiency of the skylight you choose, as this will impact your energy bills over time. Look for skylights with good insulation, Low-E glass, and good sealing to reduce heat loss.

  • Ventilation: If you want to control the amount of air entering your home through the skylight, look for skylights with venting options. This will allow you to manage your home’s temperature and airflow, making it more comfortable year-round.

  • Cost: Skylights can range in price from a few hundred to a few thousand dollars. Consider the cost of the skylight, installation, and any necessary modifications to your roof when choosing a skylight for your home.

    • Considering these factors, you can ensure that you choose the right skylight for your home and get the most out of your investment.

Skylight Installation Process

Installing a skylight can be a complex and involved process, but it can also be a significant investment in your home’s comfort, energy efficiency, and aesthetic. Here is a step-by-step guide to the skylight installation process:


  • Preparation: Before installation, ensure that you have all the necessary tools and materials, including a skylight, flashing kit, roofing cement, and nails. It’s also important to check your roof and make any necessary repairs before installation.

  • Measure and Cut Hole: The next step is to measure and cut a hole in the roof where the skylight will be installed. This hole should be smaller than the skylight, allowing for a tight seal around the edges.

  • Install Flashing: The flashing is a critical component of the skylight installation as it prevents water from leaking through the roof. The flashing should be installed around the hole’s perimeter and sealed with roofing cement.

  • Install Skylight: The skylight should be carefully set into the hole and secured with roofing nails. The skylight should be placed on top of the flashing and sealed around the edges with roofing cement to prevent water from leaking.

  • Connect to Interior: Once the skylight is securely installed, it should be connected to the home’s interior. This involves installing a frame or drywall around the skylight, connecting it to the electrical system if necessary, and adding any necessary insulation to prevent heat loss.

  • Finish the Roof: Finally, the roof should be finished and sealed around the skylight to prevent water from leaking. This may involve installing shingles, tar paper, or other roofing materials, depending on your roof type.

  • Final Inspection: After the installation is complete, a final inspection should be conducted to ensure everything is installed correctly and working. This includes checking the roof around the skylight for leaks, the skylight for proper ventilation, and the electrical connection is working.

    • Installing a skylight can be challenging, but with the right tools and materials, it can be a rewarding investment in your home. If you need more confidence in your ability to install a skylight, hiring a professional is recommended to ensure the job is done correctly and safely.

Maintenance of Skylights

Maintaining your skylights is essential to ensure they continue to provide natural light and ventilation and to prevent leaks and damage. Here are some critical steps to keep your skylights in good condition:


  • Clean Regularly: Dirt and debris can build up on the skylight, reducing the amount of light that enters your home. Regular cleaning with a soft cloth and mild soap can help keep the skylight clear and bright.

  • Check for Leaks: Leaks can be caused by damage to the roof, the skylight, or improper installation. Regularly inspect the roof and interior around the skylight for signs of leaks, such as water stains or mold.

  • Inspect the Seals: The seals around the skylight are designed to prevent water from leaking through the roof. Over time, these seals can become brittle and crack, allowing water to enter the home. Regularly inspect the seals and replace them if they are damaged or worn.

  • Check Ventilation: Proper ventilation is essential to prevent moisture buildup and regulate the temperature in your home. Regularly inspect the skylight and the vents to ensure they are functioning correctly and that air can circulate freely.

  • Check Glass: The glass in the skylight can become scratched or damaged over time, reducing the amount of light that enters your home. Regularly inspect the glass for cracks or damage and replace it if necessary.

  • Check for Insects: Insects can get into the home through the skylight, causing damage or causing a health hazard. Regularly inspect the skylight and the vents for signs of insects and take steps to prevent them from entering the home.

  • Professional Maintenance: Regular professional maintenance can help ensure that your skylights are functioning correctly and that any issues are addressed before they become more significant problems. This may include checking the roof and skylight for leaks, inspecting the seals and vents, and cleaning the glass.

    • By following these steps, you can ensure that your skylights continue to provide natural light and ventilation and remain in good condition for years to come.
